The Air Quality Index (AQI) is used for reporting daily air quality. Different air quality levels can affect your health.

Health Recommendations Based On Your AQI

  • Good (0-50): Air quality is satisfactory with little to no risk.
  • Moderate (51-100): Acceptable quality, but some pollutants may affect sensitive individuals.
  • Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (101-150): General public not likely affected but sensitive groups may experience health effects.
  • Unhealthy (151-200): Everyone may begin to experience health effects.
  • Very Unhealthy (201-300): Health alert - everyone may experience more serious health effects.
  • Hazardous (300+): Health warnings of emergency conditions.
